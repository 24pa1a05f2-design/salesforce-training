## 1. What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.  
It is a technology or software used by companies to manage their relationships and interactions with customers.

CRM helps businesses:
- Store customer information
- Track sales and communication
- Improve customer service
- Build strong customer relationships
- Sending emails/messagess


## 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce ?

Companies use Salesforce because it helps them in:
- Customer Management
- Increase Sales
- Automate Business Processes
- Better customer Service
- Marketing Automation

Salesforce helps companies work more efficiently and build better relationships with customers.


## 3. Salesforce Core Concepts

### Account
An Account represents a company, organization, or customer business in Salesforce.

Example:
- TCS
- Infosys
- Amazon

Accounts store important business information such as:
- Company name
- Address
- Phone number
- Industry type


### Contact
A Contact represents a person associated with an Account.

Example:
- Employee
- Customer
- Manager

Contacts contain details such as:
- Name
- Email
- Phone number
- Job title

A Contact is usually linked to an Account.


### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal or business opportunity.

It helps companies track:
- Sales progress
- Deal value
- Expected closing date

Example:
If a company is planning to purchase software from a business, that sales deal is tracked as an Opportunity in Salesforce.

Opportunities help sales teams manage and close deals effectively.
